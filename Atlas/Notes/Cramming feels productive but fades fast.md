Cramming works, briefly. Massed practice produces strong short-term recall and a convincing feeling of mastery, which is exactly why people prefer it: the payoff is immediate and visible. The cost (rapid forgetting) arrives after the exam, invisible.

This is the honest counterweight to [[Spaced practice beats cramming]]: if you only need the material once, tomorrow, cramming is rational.
